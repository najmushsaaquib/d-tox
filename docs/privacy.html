D-Tox

Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

TL;DR

D-Tox does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data. Your settings stay on your device. Nothing leaves your browser.

What We Collect

Nothing. D-Tox does not collect any personal information, browsing history, or usage data.

What We Store Locally

D-Tox saves your toggle preferences and settings using Chrome's local storage API (chrome.storage.local). This data:

  • Lives entirely on your device
  • Is never transmitted to any server
  • Is never shared with any third party
  • Contains only your on/off toggle preferences — no personal identifiers

Permissions & Why We Need Them

  • storage — Save and restore your toggle settings locally on your device
  • activeTab — Detect when you are on a YouTube tab so settings can be applied
  • tabs — Open the welcome page once on first install
  • Host permissions (youtube.com) — Run the content script that hides selected YouTube elements

Third-Party Links

D-Tox includes optional links to third-party services — PayPal (for financial support), Google Forms (for feature requests and bug reports), and GitHub. These links are only activated when you explicitly click them. D-Tox does not interact with these services automatically or send any data to them on your behalf.

When you choose to visit these links, you are subject to their respective privacy policies.

Analytics & Tracking

D-Tox contains no analytics, tracking pixels, or telemetry of any kind. We do not know how many people use the extension, which features are enabled, or when the extension is active.

Children's Privacy

D-Tox does not knowingly collect data from anyone, including children under 13. Since we collect no data at all, this is not a concern.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be published at this URL with a revised "last updated" date. Since D-Tox is open source, all changes are also visible in the GitHub repository.
